NotePrompt:

how are models used in academic disciplines? give an example or two of common models in at least 20 academic disciplines in a formatted list of markdown code with a linked reference for each model that provides more extensive detail

Use of Models Across Academic Disciplines

1. Physics

  • Standard Model of Particle Physics – describes fundamental particles and forces
    https://home.cern/science/physics/standard-model
  • Newtonian Mechanics – classical model of motion
    https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/newton/

2. Chemistry

  • Molecular Orbital Theory – models electron behavior in molecules
    https://chem.libretexts.org/Bookshelves/Physical_and_Theoretical_Chemistry_Textbook_Maps/Molecular_Orbital_Theory
  • Arrhenius Equation – models reaction rates
    https://www.britannica.com/science/Arrhenius-equation

3. Biology

  • Central Dogma of Molecular Biology – information flow in cells
    https://www.nature.com/scitable/topicpage/central-dogma-of-molecular-biology-373/
  • Lotka–Volterra Model – predator–prey population dynamics
    https://mathworld.wolfram.com/Lotka-VolterraEquations.html

4. Economics

  • Supply and Demand Model – market equilibrium
    https://www.investopedia.com/terms/l/law-of-supply-demand.asp
  • IS–LM Model – macroeconomic equilibrium
    https://www.econlib.org/library/Enc/ISLMModel.html

5. Psychology

  • Cognitive Behavioral Model – thoughts, emotions, behavior interaction
    https://beckinstitute.org/about-beck/what-is-cognitive-behavioral-therapy/
  • Big Five Personality Model – personality trait structure
    https://www.apa.org/monitor/julaug04/personality

6. Sociology

  • Structural Functionalism – society as interrelated systems
    https://www.britannica.com/topic/structural-functionalism
  • Conflict Theory – power and inequality model
    https://www.britannica.com/topic/conflict-theory

7. Political Science

  • Median Voter Theorem – voting behavior model
    https://www.britannica.com/topic/median-voter-theorem
  • Rational Choice Model – political decision-making
    https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/rational-choice/

8. Anthropology

  • Cultural Evolution Models – transmission of cultural traits
    https://royalsocietypublishing.org/doi/10.1098/rstb.2010.0232
  • Kinship Models – social relationship structures
    https://www.anthroencyclopedia.com/entry/kinship

9. Linguistics

  • Generative Grammar – language structure modeling
    https://www.britannica.com/topic/generative-grammar
  • Phonological Feature Models – sound pattern representation
    https://linguapress.com/esl/grammar/phonology.htm

10. Computer Science

  • Turing Machine – computation model
    https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/turing-machine/
  • Neural Networks – machine learning model
    https://www.ibm.com/think/topics/neural-networks

11. Mathematics

  • Graph Theory Models – relational structures
    https://mathworld.wolfram.com/GraphTheory.html
  • Stochastic Models – probabilistic systems
    https://www.britannica.com/science/stochastic-process

12. Statistics

  • Linear Regression Model – relationship estimation
    https://www.itl.nist.gov/div898/handbook/pmd/section1/pmd141.htm
  • Bayesian Models – probabilistic inference
    https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/bayesian-epistemology/

13. Medicine

  • Biopsychosocial Model – health determinants
    https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK396/
  • Disease Progression Models – clinical forecasting
    https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/medicine-and-dentistry/disease-progression-model

14. Public Health

  • Epidemiological SIR Model – disease spread
    https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5348083/
  • Social Determinants of Health Model
    https://www.cdc.gov/socialdeterminants/index.htm

15. Education

  • Bloom’s Taxonomy – learning objectives
    https://cft.vanderbilt.edu/guides-sub-pages/blooms-taxonomy/
  • Constructivist Learning Model
    https://www.simplypsychology.org/constructivism.html

16. Philosophy

  • Argumentation Models (Toulmin Model)
    https://www.britannica.com/topic/Toulmin-model
  • Possible Worlds Semantics
    https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/possible-worlds/

17. Geography

  • Central Place Theory – urban settlement patterns
    https://www.britannica.com/topic/central-place-theory
  • Gravity Model – spatial interaction
    https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/social-sciences/gravity-model

18. Environmental Science

  • Climate Models (GCMs) – climate system simulation
    https://www.metoffice.gov.uk/weather/climate/climate-models
  • Ecosystem Models – energy and nutrient flows
    https://www.britannica.com/science/ecosystem-model

19. Engineering

  • Finite Element Models – structural analysis
    https://www.ansys.com/solutions/what-is-finite-element-analysis
  • Control System Models – system regulation
    https://www.britannica.com/technology/control-system

20. Law

  • Legal Reasoning Models – precedent-based inference
    https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/legal-reasoning/
  • Economic Analysis of Law
    https://www.britannica.com/topic/law-and-economics

21. History

  • Cliometric Models – quantitative historical analysis
    https://www.britannica.com/topic/cliometrics
  • Periodization Models – historical structuring
    https://www.historyskills.com/historical-knowledge/periodisation/

22. Theology / Religious Studies

  • Hermeneutical Models – interpretation frameworks
    https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/hermeneutics/
  • Comparative Religion Models
    https://www.britannica.com/topic/comparative-religion
